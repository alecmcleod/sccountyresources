About Santa Cruz County Resources

Our programs began on the corner of Coral and River Street in Santa Cruz in the 1980s as a loosely affiliated group of faith-based and secular organizations providing basic food and shelter to people experiencing homelessness. They have evolved into four emergency and transitional housing shelters for nearly 230 people, a medical clinic, and a 12-bed medical respite care facility.

People experiencing homelessness are the most vulnerable members of our community. They lack access to health care as well as housing, job opportunities, community connections, and other advantages. They often have chronic illnesses made worse by their challenging living conditions.
